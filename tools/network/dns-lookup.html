    How This Works

    DNS queries are sent directly from your browser to Cloudflare's DNS-over-HTTPS (DoH) endpoint at cloudflare-dns.com/dns-query. DoH wraps DNS in standard HTTPS, allowing lookups to work from a browser context — traditional DNS (UDP port 53) is inaccessible to web pages. This means results come from Cloudflare's resolvers, not your local ISP's DNS, and may differ from what dig or nslookup show on your machine.

    • A / AAAA — IPv4 and IPv6 address records; the primary way domain names map to IPs.
    • MX — mail exchanger records specifying which servers handle incoming email. Lower priority value = higher preference.
    • TXT — arbitrary text used for SPF (anti-spoofing), DKIM public keys, domain ownership verification, and more.
    • CNAME — canonical name alias; the domain points to another domain. CNAMEs cannot coexist with other records at the zone apex (@).
    • TTL — Time To Live in seconds; how long resolvers cache this record before re-querying the authoritative server.
