    UUID Generator

    Generate v4 UUIDs individually or in bulk

      How it works

      UUIDs (Universally Unique Identifiers) are 128-bit values standardized in RFC 9562. Version 4 — what this tool generates — fills 122 of those bits with cryptographically random data; the remaining 6 bits encode the version and variant markers.

      This tool uses the browser's native crypto.randomUUID() — no external library, no server call. The output is formatted as xxxxxxxx-xxxx-4xxx-yxxx-xxxxxxxxxxxx where 4 indicates v4 and y is one of 8, 9, a, or b (variant bits).

      Collision odds: you'd need to generate approximately 2.7 × 1018 UUIDs before reaching a 50% probability of any two matching — effectively impossible in practice. UUIDs are widely used as database primary keys and distributed system identifiers precisely because they require no central coordinator to guarantee uniqueness.
