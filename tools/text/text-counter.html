    How counting works

    • Characters — uses JavaScript's .length property, which counts UTF-16 code units. Most characters = 1; emoji and characters above U+FFFF = 2 (surrogate pairs). This matches what most text fields and databases report.
    • Words — splits on whitespace runs (/\S+/g matches). Consecutive spaces count as one delimiter.
    • Sentences — heuristic: splits on . ! ? followed by a space or end of string. Abbreviations and decimal numbers may produce false splits.
    • Paragraphs — counts non-empty blocks separated by one or more blank lines.
    • Reading time — estimated at 200 words per minute (the commonly cited average for silent reading of English prose).
    • Bytes (UTF-8) — uses TextEncoder to get the true byte count. ASCII is 1 byte per char; most Latin-extended and common symbols are 2–3 bytes; emoji are typically 4 bytes.
